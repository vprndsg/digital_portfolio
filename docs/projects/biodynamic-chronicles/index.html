Biodynamic Chronicles

A daily wine-and-moon forecast designed like a living magazine, with a forecast rail on the left and a story feed on the right.

I took a weird niche idea and turned it into a habit-forming digital object: part forecast, part editorial feed, part brand world.

Presented as a working case study, with more estate-brand polish than concept-page theater.

Challenge

Biodynamic wine culture can feel opaque, mystical, and slightly unserious from the outside. The challenge was turning that into something a stranger could understand in seconds and still want to explore.

Deliverables

  • Interactive microsite
  • Forecast UX
  • Editorial feed system
  • Motion and media behavior
  • Portfolio-ready case study

Approach

How the project was structured.

1 / Approach

Anchor the premise with a usable forecast

The forecast column gives today's reading and the next three days first, so the concept feels structured before it gets atmospheric.

2 / Approach

Make the story feed feel like a publication

Instead of generic blog cards, the right rail acts like a magazine feed with a wine write-up, an adventure story, and a photo essay.

3 / Approach

Treat interaction as proof of craft

Layered image reveals, expandable stories, and a lightbox turn the page from a mockup into a usable digital object.
